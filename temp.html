City Latitude vs. Maximum Temperature


When the latitude coordinates for a random city start increasing from -40 towards 80, there is a shift in the maximum temperature a city sees. Cities with latitudinal coordinates from -40 to 20 have a maximum temperature between 60- and 100-degrees Fahrenheits due to their relatively close position to the Equator. As the longitudinal coordinates keep increasing, however, the maximum temperature continues to drop and reaches a low point of -60-degrees Fahrenheit, as the higher the latitude, the closer a city is to the North Pole.
