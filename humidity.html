City Latitude vs. Humidity


Humidity does not seem to correlate to a city’s latitude. While one may think humidity would increase the closer a city is to the equator, the relationship between percent humidity and a city’s latitude is completely random and scattered for the cities chosen in this study. There is some light clustering of percent humidity values between 80% and 100%, but they are spread through all the latitudinal coordinates. Many environmental factors can influence humidity, including recent precipitation, a city’s temperature, altitude, and amount of natural vegetation in an area. Increasing the number of cities could help elucidate if there is a relationship between humidity and latitude not seen in the smaller sample.
